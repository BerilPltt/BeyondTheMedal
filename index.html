Beyond the Medal: The Economical, Social and Cultural Impact of Women in the Olympics

A Comprehensive Research Study on Gender Equality in Olympic Sports

Research Introduction

Women's Olympic journeys have been defined by decades of struggle, marginalization, and, ultimately, empowerment. Female athletes' involvement in the Olympic Games has been historically influenced by broader socioeconomic factors such as economic growth, political systems, and cultural perspectives on gender equality.

This research aims to provide critical insights into the complex landscape of gender representation in international sports, exploring the multifaceted factors that shape female athletes' participation in the Olympic Games.

Research Questions

Political and Economic Determinants

Investigate the influence of the Democracy Index, Gender Inequality Index (GII), and GDP per capita on female athlete representation in the Paris 2024 Olympics.

Gender Equity in Sports

Examine the correlation between gender equity in sports participation across Olympic disciplines and the Gender Inequality Index of participating nations.

Economic Progress and Representation

Analyze the patterns in female athlete representation over time and their relationship to nations' economic growth as measured by GDP.

Research Objectives

The primary objectives of this research are to:

  • Elucidate how societal constructs dictate opportunities for women in sports
  • Identify which Olympic sports demonstrate greater gender inclusivity
  • Understand the relationship between economic growth and female athlete representation
  • Provide insights into the ongoing struggle for gender equality in global sports

By examining these critical aspects, the research aims to contribute to a deeper understanding of the social, economic, and cultural factors that shape women's participation in the Olympic Games.

Research Significance

This study offers a comprehensive analysis of gender representation in Olympic sports, providing valuable insights into the complex interplay of economic, social, and cultural factors that influence female athletes' opportunities and achievements.
